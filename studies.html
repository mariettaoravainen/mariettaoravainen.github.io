1st Year (2022–2023)

In fall 2022, I completed my first group project on the hardware-oriented track: a physical tic-tac-toe game built with an Arduino circuit board and programmed in C++ using the Arduino IDE.

In spring 2023, we developed a banking system simulator using C++ and the Qt framework. The system integrated a physical card reader, offering hands-on experience with both software logic and hardware interaction.

After this year, I decided to specialize in software development.

2nd Year (2023–2024)

In fall 2023, our team developed Moviehub, a full-stack movie review platform. The frontend was built with React, and the backend was implemented using Node.js and Java. Users could register, browse films, and leave reviews. We also created a promotional video and published it on YouTube.

In spring 2024, I participated in the international Joffer project in collaboration with Fontys University of Applied Sciences(Netherlands). The project focused on creating a job search platform with both web and mobile versions. Our team developed the mobile client, while the Fontys team handled the backend and the OAMK team implemented the web interface.

3rd Year (2024–2025)

The fall semester of 2024 was mainly theory-based.

In spring 2025, I began an exchange program at Hochschule der Medien in Stuttgart, Germany. The curriculum combined theoretical courses with project-based work.The most notable project was completed in collaboration with Mercedes-Benz. Our team explored the question: "How can people learn to make more sustainable EV charging choices?" The project included research, user interviews, and the design of a prototype/mockup related to smart charging.
